Activities undertaken by the Samithi

Medical Activities

The Samithi renders service to the poor and helpless patients in and around the locality. The devoted and self-sacrificing services of other eminent doctors as well as lay devotees have enabled these activities to be carried out.

Blood donation camps

Blood donation camps have been organized by the Samithi, in association with the Nalgonda Blood Bank, since 2006 on the birth anniversary of Swami Vivekananda. The Samithi has beed awarded the Best blood donation camp organizer award for 2011 by the Government of India.

ENT and General Medical Camps

Six ENT and General medical camps have been conducted so far catering to the people in and around Huzurnagar.

Eye Camps

We have conducted about 25 eye camps in association with government doctors and government recognised private doctors. We also provide free medicine to the needy. To those requiring further treatment or operations, we have assisted in the patients getting the required medical help from the government and personal assistance provided during surgery.

  • Garedelpalli

Relief Activities

The Samithi undertakes instant relief operations during natural calamities like fire, cyclone, tsunami, earthquake, floods, etc and man-made disasters. Primary relief operations are undertaken immediately by providing food, clothing, utensils, etc.

Godavari Flood Relief

We surveyed Badrachalam area during the Godavari floods. Koonavaram and Sitammaplli villages were identified as affected areas with help of MRO of Badrachalam. Lungi, Saree, Blankets and Towels worth Rs.20,000 were distributed to the needy.

Krishna Flood Relief

We identified Mehboob nagar, kollapur as an affected area of the Krishna floods. We distributed groceries worth of 1.5 Lakh Rupess to about 150 families of the area. Also we distributed food to about 5-6 villages in and around Huzurnagar, over a period of 2 days.

Corona Pandemic Relief Activities

During the corona pandemic, we surveyed the affected regions in and around Huzurnagar. Relief activity was carried out in ward number 1 of Huzurnagar. Groceries worth Rs.25000 were distributed to 50 families.

Being a non-government organization dedicated to the service of God in man, the Math is entirely dependent on the financial help extended to it by the generous public. All donations are thankfully accepted and acknowledged.

Rural Development Activities

Drinking water service

An RO plant is established at the samithi that provides drinking water service to the poor. The plant was set up at a cost of Rs. 2 Lakhs, donated to us by the Ramakrishna Mutt of Hyderabad. Water from this plant is made available at the cost of Rs.5 per liter.
